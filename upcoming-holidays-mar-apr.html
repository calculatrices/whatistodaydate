Understanding Holiday Types

Holidays in the U.S. can vary significantly in their observance:

  • Federal Holidays: Federal government offices are closed nationwide, with most banks and post offices closed. Many businesses and schools are also closed during this time.
  • State Holidays: Holidays observed by certain states, meaning that government offices and schools for that state may be closed. However, they are not observed nationwide.
  • Local Observances: Holidays or observances observed only in a specific city, county, or region.
  • Observances: Widely recognized days of celebration, but generally not days off from work or school. These are often cultural or awareness days.
  • Religious/Cultural Holidays: Holidays associated with specific religious or cultural traditions. Businesses may or may not be closed, and individuals observe them based on personal preference.

Planning Your Spring Around Holidays

Knowing these dates allows you to:

  • Plan your vacation strategically to make the most of any long weekend.
  • Anticipate potential delays in services due to government office or bank closures.
  • Attend cultural celebrations or state events.
  • Coordinate business activities to minimize disruptions.
