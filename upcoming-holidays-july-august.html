Understanding Holiday Types

Holidays in the U.S. can vary significantly in their observance:

  • Federal Holidays: Federal government offices are observed nationwide, and banks and post offices are closed. Some businesses and schools are also closed.
  • State Holidays: Holidays observed in some states, meaning that state government offices and schools in that state may be closed. But they are not observed nationwide.
  • Local Observances: Holidays or observances observed only in a specific city, county, or region.
  • Observances: Widely recognized days for celebration, but are not usually days off from work or school. These are often cultural or awareness days.
  • Religious/Cultural Holidays:Holidays associated with specific religious traditions. Businesses may or may not be closed, and individuals may choose to take the day off based on personal preference.

Planning Your Summer Around Holidays

  • Plan your vacation strategically to make the most of your long vacation.
  • Anticipate government bank closures or potential delays in government services.
  • Participate in cultural celebrations or community events.
  • Coordinate various plans to minimize disruptions.

This list is designed as a helpful guide to meet your planning needs. Always confirm with the organization for specific closures.
