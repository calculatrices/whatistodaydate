Understanding Holiday Types

Holidays in the U.S. can vary significantly in their observance:

  • Federal Holidays: Government offices observe federal holidays. Many banks and post offices are closed during these times.
  • State Holidays: Certain states observe these holidays, and state government offices and schools may be closed during these times. However, they are not observed nationwide.
  • Local Observances: A special day recognized for widespread celebration, but usually not a day off from work or school. These are often cultural or awareness days.
  • Observances: A special day recognized for widespread celebration, but usually not a day off from work or school. These are often cultural or awareness days.
  • Religious/Cultural Holidays: Holidays associated with specific religious or cultural traditions. Businesses may or may not be closed, and individuals may choose to take the day off based on personal preference.
