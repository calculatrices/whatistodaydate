Understanding Holiday Types

Holidays in the U.S. can vary significantly in their observance:

  • Federal Holidays: Federal holidays are days when all government offices are closed nationwide, and most banks and post offices are closed. Many businesses and schools are also closed.
  • State Holidays: Holidays observed by certain states, meaning that state government offices and schools in that state may be closed. However, they are not observed nationwide.
  • Local Observances: Holidays or observances observed only in a specific city, county, or region.
  • Observances: Widely recognized days to celebrate a specific event, but are not usually days off from work or school. These are often cultural or awareness days.
  • Religious/Cultural Holidays: These are holidays related to religious or cultural traditions. Businesses may or may not be closed, and individuals may choose to take the day off based on personal preference.

Planning Your Spring & Early Summer Around Holidays

Knowing these dates allows you to:

  • Plan your vacation strategically to make the most of any long weekend.
  • Anticipate potential delays in services due to government office or bank closures.
  • Participate in cultural celebrations or community events.
  • Coordinate business activities to minimize disruptions.
