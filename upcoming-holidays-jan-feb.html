Understanding Holiday Types

Holidays in the U.S. can vary significantly in their observance:

  • Federal Holidays: On this day, the entire country observes the day with federal government offices closed, while banks and post offices are closed. Many businesses and schools are also closed.
  • State Holidays: Government offices and schools in that state may remain closed on the day the state observes a state holiday. It is not observed nationwide.
  • Local Observances: Holidays are celebrated in certain cities or regions.
  • Observances: Days of celebration are widely recognized, but are not usually days off from work or school. They are called awareness days.
  • Religious/Cultural Holidays: Holidays tied to specific religious traditions, businesses may or may not close, but individuals may take the day off based on personal preference.

Planning Your Winter Around Holidays

Knowing these dates allows you to:

  • Plan how you can make the most of a holiday week.
  • Anticipate potential delays in services due to bank closures.
  • Participate in social community events.
  • Coordinate business activities to minimize disruptions.
